Values

This is just a starter, so I can’t tell you what your design values should be, except that you should have them. If you don’t yet, spend some time thinking about them. Articulating what it is that you value — and just as importantly, what you don’t — is critical, foundational design work. You’ve probably heard that “design is intent.” Well, knowing and being able to articulate your design values is a crucial part of making that intent clear. If you’re clear about what your values are, and you understand the problem in front of you, in the vast majority of situations, the solution is clear.

You probably shouldn’t go for values like, “keep things simple,” or “communicate clearly.” Those are universal rules of good design. Rather, think about the values that are important to this system that you wouldn’t necessarily follow in a different context — or what values might be really important somewhere else that don’t apply here.

Principles

Where design values are high-level and abstract, principles are a bit more down to earth. Design principles are, if not rules exactly, then something a bit stronger than guidelines. There may well be times and places when you should break them, but you should never break them without a good reason.

Like values, a good way to think of your design principles is in terms of the great ideas that you would follow without hesitation somewhere else, but not for this system.
